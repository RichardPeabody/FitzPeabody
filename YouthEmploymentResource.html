Purpose

Early work can build more than pocket money.

For many young people, a first job teaches confidence, punctuality, communication, resilience and responsibility. Yet many adults are unsure what is allowed, what is sensible, and what protections need to be in place.

Who it is for

Four groups need the same information, in different language.

Employers

Simple explanations of age limits, hours, permitted work, risk assessment, supervision, safeguarding and how to offer meaningful experience responsibly.

Parents

Practical reassurance on what questions to ask, what warning signs to look for, and how work can support confidence without compromising school or wellbeing.

Schools

A neutral resource to help discuss employability, local opportunities, work readiness and the difference between exploitation and proper early experience.

Young people

Clear, friendly guidance on where to start, how to approach employers, what to expect, and how to recognise a good first workplace.

What the website could include

  • Plain-English summaries of the rules for school-age work, with links to official guidance.
  • Checklists for employers before offering a young person work or work experience.
  • Parent guides covering safety, travel, hours, pay, supervision and school impact.
  • Examples of suitable first jobs and the skills each can develop.
  • Templates for simple role descriptions, permission notes and workplace expectations.
  • Stories showing how early work can shape confidence, ambition and independence.
Principles

The message is positive, but never casual about risk.

1

Legal first

The resource should not encourage shortcuts. It should help people understand local rules, official guidance and the need to check requirements properly.

2

Safeguarding always

Work must be suitable, supervised and age-appropriate. The resource should make protections feel practical rather than bureaucratic.

3

Benefits explained

The case for early work should be made clearly: confidence, manners, resilience, independence, teamwork, customer skills and pride in earning.

The goal is not to push children into work. It is to help adults create safe, useful opportunities when a young person is ready for them. Youth Employment Resource concept
