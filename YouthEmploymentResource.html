Why this exists

Getting started should not feel confusing.

Lots of young people would like to earn a little money, help out, meet people and feel useful. Lots of employers would like to offer chances too. The problem is that everyone worries about the rules, the risks and whether they are doing the right thing.

Who it is for

Different questions. Different perspectives.

Young people

Thinking about a first job? We explain where to start, what sort of work might suit you, what to ask, what to expect and how to stay safe.

Parents

Clear information on hours, travel, supervision, pay, safety and how work can build confidence without getting in the way of school or wellbeing.

Employers

Simple guidance on giving young people a good first experience while understanding age limits, suitable work, supervision and responsibilities.

Teachers & Schools

A practical place to support conversations about work, responsibility, confidence and life beyond the classroom.

How we think about it

Positive about work. Serious about safety.

1

Keep it legal

No shortcuts. The site points people towards the rules and encourages adults to check what applies locally.

2

Keep it safe

Work should be suitable, supervised and age-appropriate. Safety should feel practical, not frightening.

3

Make it worthwhile

A good first job can teach confidence, manners, teamwork, resilience, customer skills and pride in earning.

The aim is not to push children into work. It is to help young people get good first experiences when they are ready for them. Youth Employment Resource
